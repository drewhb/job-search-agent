## Voice rules — apply ruthlessly

**Use:**
- **Active voice, "I" sentences.** "I built." "I own." Not "X was built" or "responsible for."
- **Specific named artifacts.** "[Project Name]." Not "an internal tool." Real names, real numbers.
- **Numbers when they help.** Timeframes, scale, impact. Not vanity metrics.
- **Direct cause-and-effect.** "I built X *so that* Y." Logic chains, not vibes.
- **Short paragraphs.** 3–5 sentences each. White space earns the read.
- **Self-awareness about gaps.** When pivoting, name the pivot and reframe why it's the point.

**Avoid:**
- Corporate buzzwords: [list yours — e.g., "leverage," "synergize," "ecosystem," "passionate about," "thought leadership"]
- Weak openers: "I am writing to apply for…", "I am excited to submit my application…"
- [Your specific pet peeves — e.g., "Em-dashes as connective tissue — use periods instead"]
- Vague skill claims without proof. "Strong communicator" → cut it or replace with evidence.
- Apologetic / hedging language. "I think I might be a good fit" → no. State it.
- Restating resume bullets. Cover letter tells the *why* and connects 2–3 points to this role's needs.
- AI-tells: starting sentences with "Moreover," "Furthermore," "In addition."

## Length targets

- **Cover letter:** ~300–400 words. 4–5 short paragraphs. One page.
- **Recruiter reply (cold inbound):** 4–6 sentences. Warm, specific, clear next step.
- **Recruiter reply (active conversation):** match their length, slightly shorter. End with a question or proposal.
- **Resume bullet:** ~1.5 lines max. One verb, one outcome, one quantifier when possible.

## When the agent is unsure

If a draft starts to feel generic — too many adjectives, not enough proper nouns — stop and re-read the sample cover letter. Then ask: *which two or three concrete things from the user's actual work (see `memory/about-me.md`) map most directly to this specific role's stated needs?* Anchor the draft to those.

If still unsure, show the user the draft and ask which bits feel like them and which feel like AI. Iterate from there.
